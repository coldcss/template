Key Features of Ethereum 2.0

The next evolution of Ethereum brings groundbreaking improvements to scalability, security, and sustainability.

Proof of Stake

Transition from energy-intensive mining to eco-friendly staking, reducing energy consumption by ~99.95%.

Sharding

Parallel processing of transactions across 64 chains, increasing throughput to 100,000+ TPS.

Enhanced Security

Stronger cryptographic security with BLS signatures and decentralized randomness.

Lower Fees

Reduced gas fees through optimized execution and data availability solutions.

Cross-Chain Interop

Native support for cross-chain communication and asset transfers.

Modular Design

Separation of execution, consensus, and data availability layers for flexibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about Ethereum's future.

Ethereum 2.0 (now simply called Ethereum) is a major upgrade to the Ethereum blockchain that transitions it from Proof of Work (PoW) to Proof of Stake (PoS), introduces sharding for scalability, and improves security and sustainability. The key differences are energy efficiency (99.95% less energy), higher transaction throughput, and lower fees at scale.

There are several ways to stake ETH:

  • Solo staking: Run your own validator node with 32 ETH
  • Staking pools: Join a pool with less than 32 ETH (e.g., Lido, Rocket Pool)
  • Centralized exchanges: Stake through platforms like Coinbase, Kraken, or Binance
  • Staking as a service: Use providers who manage the node for you

Current APR is approximately 4-6%, paid in ETH.

Sharding is being implemented in phases. The first phase (data shards) is expected in 2023, which will significantly improve scalability by providing more data availability for rollups. Execution shards that can process transactions are planned for later phases, likely 2024 or beyond. The roadmap is iterative and may change based on research and development progress.

Your existing ETH automatically became ETH2 after The Merge in September 2022. There was no action required - it's the same asset with the same value, just now secured by Proof of Stake instead of Proof of Work. All your ETH holdings, DeFi positions, NFTs, and other assets remained exactly the same.

Ethereum remains the dominant smart contract platform by most metrics:

  • Developer activity: Largest developer community with most active projects
  • Total Value Locked (TVL): Over 60% of all DeFi is on Ethereum
  • Security: Most battle-tested and decentralized network
  • Ecosystem: Largest collection of dApps, tools, and services

While other chains may offer lower fees currently, Ethereum's roadmap aims to match this scalability while maintaining its security and decentralization advantages.
